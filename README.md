# Shark-Attack-Analysis
![](https://github.com/onwudiweginikachukwu/Shark-Attack-Analysis/blob/main/SA.png)

## Project Overview
This project analyzes historical shark attack records to identify trends, locations, activities and outcomes that support awareness and safety planning.

## Objectives

- Analyze global shark attack trends and patterns.
- Identify the locations and shark species with the highest number of attacks.
- Examine when and how shark attacks occur.
- Create an interactive Power BI dashboard to generate actionable business insights.

## Tools and Skills Used

- Power BI
- Power Query
- DAX
- Data Cleaning and Transformation
- Dashboard Design
- Data Visualisation
- Data Analysis
- Data Storytelling
- Business Insights & Recommendations

  ## Key Insights

- Shark attacks generally increased after 2000, peaking in 2001, with another notable rise around 2016 before declining in recent years.
- The USA recorded the highest number of shark attacks globally.
- Florida was the most affected state, while New Smyrna Beach had the highest number of reported attacks.
- Legs and feet were the most frequently injured body parts.
- Most shark attacks occurred during the afternoon.
- Spinner Shark was the most commonly reported shark species in the cleaned dataset.

## Recommendations


- Prioritize marketing and safety campaigns in high-risk locations, especially the USA and Florida.
- Promote water safety awareness during the afternoon, when shark attacks are most frequent.
- Develop or market protective gear for swimmers, surfers, and other water-sport enthusiasts, focusing on leg and foot protection.
- Continuously monitor shark attack trends to support better business planning and product targeting.
